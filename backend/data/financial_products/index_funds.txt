# Index Funds - Investment Product Guide

## S&P 500 Index Funds
**Description**: Tracks the S&P 500, representing 500 largest US companies
**Historical Return**: 10% annually (long-term average)
**Risk Level**: Moderate
**Time Horizon**: 5+ years recommended
**Minimum Investment**: Varies by provider ($1-$3,000)
**Fees**: 0.03% - 0.20% expense ratio

**Recommended For**:
- Long-term investors (10+ years)
- Retirement savings
- Growth-focused portfolios
- Investors comfortable with market volatility

**Providers**: Vanguard (VOO), Fidelity (FXAIX), Schwab (SWPPX)

---

## Total Stock Market Index Funds
**Description**: Entire US stock market exposure (3,000+ companies)
**Historical Return**: 9-10% annually
**Risk Level**: Moderate
**Time Horizon**: 5+ years
**Minimum Investment**: $1-$3,000
**Fees**: 0.03% - 0.15%

**Recommended For**:
- Broad diversification seekers
- Buy-and-hold investors
- Core portfolio holding

---

## International Index Funds
**Description**: Exposure to developed and emerging markets outside US
**Historical Return**: 6-8% annually (varies by region)
**Risk Level**: Moderate to High
**Time Horizon**: 7+ years
**Fees**: 0.07% - 0.30%

**Recommended For**:
- Portfolio diversification beyond US
- Long-term global exposure
- Risk-tolerant investors

## Target-Date Funds
**Description**: Automatically adjusts stock/bond allocation as retirement date approaches
**Historical Return**: 6-9% (varies by target date)
**Risk Level**: Decreases over time (aggressive â†’ conservative)
**Time Horizon**: Matched to retirement date
**Fees**: 0.10% - 0.75%

**Recommended For**:
- Retirement savers who want "set it and forget it"
- Investors who don't want to manually rebalance
- Clear retirement timeline

**How It Works**:
- 30 years to retirement: ~90% stocks, 10% bonds
- 20 years: ~80% stocks, 20% bonds
- 10 years: ~60% stocks, 40% bonds
- At retirement: ~40% stocks, 60% bonds
