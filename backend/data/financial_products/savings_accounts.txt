# Savings & Cash Equivalents - Short-Term Investment Guide

## High-Yield Savings Accounts (HYSA)
**Description**: FDIC-insured savings accounts with competitive interest rates
**Yield**: 4-5% APY (current rates, variable)
**Risk Level**: None (FDIC insured up to $250,000)
**Liquidity**: Immediate access
**Minimum Deposit**: $0-$100

**Providers**: Ally Bank, Marcus by Goldman Sachs, Capital One 360

**Recommended For**:
- Emergency fund (3-6 months expenses)
- Short-term goals (under 1 year)
- Money needed within months
- Risk-averse savers

---

## Certificates of Deposit (CDs)
**Description**: Time-locked savings with fixed interest rate
**Yield**: 4-5.5% APY (1-5 year terms)
**Risk Level**: None (FDIC insured)
**Liquidity**: Low (early withdrawal penalties)
**Terms**: 3 months to 5 years

**Recommended For**:
- Money not needed for specific time period
- Guaranteed return
- Conservative investors
- Goals 1-5 years away

**CD Ladder Strategy**: Spread money across multiple CDs with different maturity dates for better liquidity

---

## Money Market Accounts
**Description**: Hybrid checking/savings account with higher rates
**Yield**: 4-5% APY
**Risk Level**: None (FDIC insured)
**Liquidity**: High (limited monthly transactions)
**Features**: Check writing, debit card access

**Recommended For**:
- Emergency fund with easy access
- Parking cash short-term
- Better rates than checking accounts

---

## Treasury Money Market Funds
**Description**: Mutual funds investing in short-term government securities
**Yield**: 4-5% annually
**Risk Level**: Very Low (not FDIC insured, but highly stable)
**Liquidity**: High (T+1 settlement)
**Minimum Investment**: $1-$3,000

**Recommended For**:
- Cash portion of investment portfolio
- Parking money between investments
- Slightly higher yield than savings accounts

---

## Comparison: When to Use Each

**Emergency Fund (need within days)**:
→ High-Yield Savings Account or Money Market Account

**Goal in 6-12 months (vacation, large purchase)**:
→ High-Yield Savings Account or Short-term CD

**Goal in 1-3 years (car, wedding, down payment)**:
→ CD Ladder or Short-term Bond Fund

**Goal in 3-5 years (medium-term)**:
→ Mix of CDs and Conservative Bond Funds

**Goal 5+ years (retirement, long-term)**:
→ Index Funds (stocks) and Bond Funds
